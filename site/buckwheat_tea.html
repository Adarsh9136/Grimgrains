buckwheat tea

1 teapot — 10 minutes

A friend from Japan gave us some soba cha そば茶 last month, it's something we've had before but that we never thought of making ourselves. It's a type of tea that is served in some soba shops, as a companion drink to buckwheat noodles. It has a subtle nutty flavor, and is perfect to drink after dinner or later at night as it doesn't have any caffeine.



I like to roast the groats as I need them, but it's possible to prepare a larger batch ahead of time. The roasted buckwheat groats will store for a few months if kept in a cool and dry place. When roasting a larger batch, use a larger pan so that the groats don't sit atop of each other. The groats must be in a single layer so they can cook evenly.

Re-using the softened groats: It's possible to re-use the softened groats by pouring more water onto them, although the flavor will not be as strong. If you like to minimize food waste, it's possible to incorporate the soft groats in other meals. For example, you can mix it into rice, cookies, breads etc.

roasting

buckwheat groats 2 tbsp
  • Bring a pan to medium heat, add 2 tbsp of raw buckwheat groats.
  • Toast the buckwheat_groats until they're fragrant and lightly browned, all while stirring constantly. Do this for about 5-6 minutes. Then, lower the heat and continue to toast the groats until they turn a deep brown color. Continue stirring, and watch them so they don't burn.
  • Transfer to a bowl, let cool.

infusion

buckwheat groats 2 tbsp, roasted
water 250ml, hot
  • Add 2 tbsp of roasted buckwheat groats into the tea strainer of a teapot, and pour 3 cups of hot water overtop. Let tea infuse for 5 minutes.
