veggie pate

4 blocks — 60 minutes

Végé pâté is Quebec staple food. It's great with veggies, on crackers and in sandwiches. Purchasing it pre-made can be expensive, and not all brands are good.

Substitutions

A lot of végé pâté recipes call for whole wheat flour, I've made pâté with it before with great results. If you don't have oats, you can use whole wheat flour instead.

preparation

sunflower seeds 1 cup
sweet potatoes 1 1/2 cups, grated
carrots 1, grated
canola oil 2 tbsp
yellow onion 1
garlic 3 cloves
rolled oats 3/4 cup, ground
soy sauce 3 tbsp
lemon juice 1 tsp
thyme 1 tsp
oregano 1 tsp
black pepper to taste
water 1/3 cup
  • Soak 1 cup of raw unsalted sunflower seeds in water for 2 hours. Then, drain, rinse well and transfer to a big bowl. Puree the seeds using an immersion blender. Set aside.
  • Preheat oven to 350F.
  • Skin, and grate enough potatoes to fill 1 1/2 cups. Also, grate 1 large carrot. Then transfer both the grated carrot and potatoes into the big bowl with the sunflower seeds.
  • Chop 1 yellow onion and 3 garlic cloves. Bring a pan to medium heat, add vegetable oil and the chopped yellow onion. Sautée until browned, then add the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Pour contents of pan into the big bowl.
  • Pulse 3/4 cup of rolled oats into a fine powder using an immersion blender or mortar and pestle, this should give you 1/2 cup of oat powder. Add oat powder into the big bowl.
  • Add 2 tbsp of canola oil, 3 tbsp of soy sauce, 1 tsp of lemon juice, 1 tsp of dried oregano, 1 tsp of dried thyme and 1/3 cup of water to the rest of the ingredients. Blend everything using an immersion blender, then add a pinch of black pepper and 1/4 cup of nutritional yeast. Mix it in with a spoon.
  • Pour mixture into a greased 8x8 baking dish, and bake for 50 minutes. Let cool before serving. Keeps for a week in the fridge, and even longer if frozen.
