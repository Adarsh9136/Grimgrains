purple cauliflower

A type of cauliflower, the purple color in this cauliflower is caused by the presence anthocyanins, water-soluble pigments that are found in many other plants and plant-based products, such as red cabbage and red wine. Cauliflower is high in vitamin C. Eating ½ cup of cauliflower has been shown to increase iron absorption from plant foods by as much as four to six times.
