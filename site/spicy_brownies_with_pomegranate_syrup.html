spicy brownies with pomegranate syrup

24 servings — 60 minutes

A classic brownie recipe with a kick, topped with a chili-infused pomegranate syrup.

I put a LOT of chili pepper flakes in these. You can omit the chilis in the batter, but I recommend keeping it in the pomegranate syrup. It's delicious and won't be the same without it.



You can vary the fruit juice for the topping. Making reductions of fruit juices is very easy, it adds a touch of fancy without too much effort.

Substitutions

Fat : Use 113 g (~1/2 cup) vegan butter instead of oil. It's possible to use less fat, but you can only substitute half the amount of fat before it affects the texture. For example, you can use 45 ml (1/8th cup) of vegetable oil with 60 g (45 ml) of pumpkin puree.

Chocolate : Use unsweetened cocoa powder instead of bar chocolate, for every 30 g (1 oz) of chocolate add 45 g (3 tbsp) of cocoa powder plus 15 g (1 tbsp) of vegan butter.

Final tip: To prevent burning the bottom of your brownies, place the pan on a preheated cookie sheet. Brownie tips courtesy of the kitchen project.

brownies

canola oil 90 ml
dark chocolate 250 g
flax seeds 45 g
water 135 ml
brown sugar 75 g
granulated sugar 100 g
chili pepper flakes 30 g
cayenne pepper powder 2.5 g
salt 1.25 g
all purpose flour 140 g
  • Preheat oven to 180 °C (350 °F).
  • Melt 90 ml of canola oil (or 113 g | 1/2 cup vegan butter) and 250 g of coarsely chopped unsweetened chocolate together. The best way to do this is in a double boiler or any small pan placed over a pot of gently simmering water. If you're an experienced baker, you can place the oil/butter and chocolate directly in a saucepan over a low flame. Be sure to stir the mixture constantly. Remove from heat, transfer to a bowl and let cool.
  • While the chocolate is cooling down, in a small bowl add 45 g (3 tbsp) of ground flax seeds with 135 ml (9 tbsp) of water. Let thicken for 5 minutes.
  • Pour the flax 'eggs' into the melted chocolate/oil, then add 75 g (5 tbsp) of brown sugar and 100 g (1/2 cup) of granulated sugar. Stir well, without overmixing.
  • Add 30 g (1-2 tbsp) of chili pepper flakes and 2.5 g (1/2 tsp) of cayenne pepper powder, 1.25 g of salt and 140 g (1 cup) of all purpose flour and mix just long enough to blend them.
  • Pour into a greased 8X8 baking dish. To improve the flavor of the brownies, place the unbaked batter in refrigerator overnight (or a few hours). If you don't want to wait, place baking dish in oven and bake for ~20 minutes. Add 2 minutes if using vegan butter. To check if it's ready, insert a toothpick into the center, if it comes out with a few moist crumbs attached to it, it's ready. Remove from oven and let cool. Cut in 24 small squares.

syrup

chili pepper flakes 5 g
cayenne pepper powder 5 g
pomegranate juice 475 ml
  • Pour 475 ml (2 cups) of unsweetened pomegranate juice into a pot with 5 g (1 tsp) chili pepper flakes and 5 g (1 tsp) cayenne pepper powder. Bring to a boil, lower to medium-high heat and leave for up to 1h or until liquid has been reduced to half.
  • Let cool, the syrup will thicken when cooled.

topping

pomegranate 2
  • Top brownies with the seeds, of two fresh pomegranates (read about a technique on how to remove the seeds) and drizzle with the chili-infused syrup!
